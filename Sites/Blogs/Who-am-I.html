Who am I?

Introduction

When I first thought of writing this I thought I would do this introspective, philosophical sort of thing. In the end I decided to settle on something simpler. My name, to put it bluntly, is Daya Singh, a first year (as of writing) mathematics student at University College London. I take interest in drawing, doing stand-up comedy, logic and coding (badly).

Early Life

I was born in London, a nice city filled with not-so-nice people (such as myself). Skipping ahead beyond the boring bits, I did Design & Technology, Computer Science, History, French and Triple science (aside from of course the more obvious ones). Skipping adead into my not so early life, I took Maths, Further Maths, Physics and Chemistry A levels. I also took STEP II and III.

Now

Currently I am an undergrad mathematics student at UCL. I spend most of my time playing pool in the institute bar, doing stand-up comedy shows, and a minor little thing called studying; maths isn't going to... erm... maths itself. I enjoy algebra, analysis, and logic; with luck I should be taking some philosophy electives

Stuff I made up to make this post interesting

At the age of 5 I was invited by President Barack Obama to speak in Congress, and as a result, I single-handedly solved the financial banking crisis of 2008. In my spare time I play russian roulette over the phone with Boris Johnson. Some of my time travel exploits involve stopping the meteorite hitting the earth in late January of 1998, preventing the production of a sequel to Batman and Robin, and the murder of Jason Todd (who do you think rigged the vote?).
